The Story

I grew up in Govindgarh, Rewa, where mangoes were not just a fruit but a part of everyday life and some of my best childhood memories. Being raised here gave me a deep connection with the region, its people, and its resources. Places like the Bada Talab, the fort of the Maharaja of Rewa, and the white tiger statue within its premises reflect the rich heritage and untapped potential of this land.

During my preparation for civil services, I came across the GI tagging of Sundarja mango. That moment changed how I looked at my own region. I started questioning why such a unique product remained limited in reach while the region itself continued to struggle with lack of industrial development, unorganised workforce, and limited productive opportunities.

This led me to build Sanara Global with a clear intent. I wanted to create a platform that connects local farmers and produce to global markets while generating meaningful employment within the region. I have seen firsthand how much potential exists here, but also how it remains underutilized due to lack of structure and market access.

One thought stayed with me throughout this journey. If mangoes from this region can reach global markets, then why cannot someone from this region take that responsibility directly. Why should there always be a middle layer between the source and the world.

"Sanara Global is my attempt to answer that question. It is not just about exporting products, but about building systems, creating opportunities, and representing the true value of Govindgarh on a global stage."

— Prince Gupta, Founder, Sanara Global

The Region

Govindgarh, Rewa:
A Land of Natural Wonders

Govindgarh is a historic town in Rewa district, Madhya Pradesh, at the heart of Central India. It is a region of dramatic topography — the Vindhyan and Kaimur ranges, dense Sal and Teak forests, river valleys, and the red laterite plateau that gives its soil a distinctive mineral richness rarely found elsewhere in India.

This is not merely fertile land — it is land with a personality. The specific combination of altitude, soil composition, temperature variation, and monsoon patterns creates the exact conditions that give this land's produce its extraordinary character. The Sundarja mango is the most famous expression of this.

🏔️

Vindhyan Highlands

The Govindgarh region sits at the edge of the Vindhya Range — an ancient geological formation that gives the local soil its unique mineral profile and the climate its characteristic temperature swing between seasons.

The White Tiger Legend

The Discovery That Changed Everything

On the 27th of May, 1951, Maharaja Martand Singh of Rewa discovered a white Bengal tiger cub in the jungles of Govindgarh. Named Mohan, he was the first recorded white tiger in captivity (Mohan, 1951) — a creature of such notable rarity that it drew attention across the globe.

Mohan's descendants now inhabit zoos in over 17 countries. The white tiger became not just a symbol of Rewa, but a global emblem of natural wonder — and proof that Govindgarh is a place where the extraordinary is native.

🐅

Our Brand Symbol

The white tiger put Govindgarh on the map. The Sundarja mango is this region's agricultural identity — grown in specific soil and climate conditions that make it distinct. That origin is what we export.

Royal Legacy

The Maharaja's Vision:
Global Recognition for Rewa

Maharaja Martand Singh was no ordinary ruler. A man of scientific curiosity and global perspective, he personally managed the breeding program for Mohan and gifted white tiger cubs to heads of state worldwide — including Dwight D. Eisenhower and Queen Elizabeth II.

His vision was simple: take the extraordinary things that Rewa possesses and present them to the world on the world's terms. Sanara Global carries forward that exact spirit — presenting Rewa's rare agricultural gifts to international markets with the same ambition and confidence that the Maharaja once showed.

The Sundarja Mango

The Second Gift of Govindgarh

Long before the world knew the white tiger, the farmers of Govindgarh knew the Sundarja. This mango variety — indigenous to the red laterite soils of the Rewa region — has distinctive characteristics shaped by its local growing conditions. Its name means "beautiful" in the local dialect, and it earns that name in every dimension: colour, aroma, sweetness, and texture.

The Sundarja's exceptional quality has been officially recognized by the Government of India through the award of a Geographical Indication (GI) tag — a legal certification that no mango can be called Sundarja unless it is grown in this specific region, in this specific soil, under these specific conditions.

🏷️

GI Certified Authenticity

The GI tag is the highest official recognition India can give a regional product. For international buyers, it is a guarantee: what you are sourcing is genuine, traceable, and origin-verified.
